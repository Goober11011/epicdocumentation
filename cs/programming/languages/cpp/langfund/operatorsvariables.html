Operators

There are many different ways of using each operator. Below will list out different operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division and more. Each type may have more then one way of doing that operation. Whenever 'operator 1'/'operator 2' is shown in the actual code you would only use one operator.
Ex:

+/- means + OR - not use +/- in a program

  • Addition/Subtraction
    • z = x +/- y

      • This assigns z to whatever x +/- y is. You could also do z = z +/- x to assign z to itself +/- x.

    • z +/-= x

      • This assigns z to z +/- x

    • z++/++z or z--/--z

      • Both of these assign z to z +/- 1

  • Multiplication/Division
    • z = x * y or z = x / y

      • This assigns z to x multiplied or divided by y. You could also do z = z * x or z = z / x to assign z to z multiplied or divided by x

    • z *= x or z /= x

      • This assigns z to z multiplied or divided itself by x

Variables

  • Short

    • Small integer which a range of -32,768 to 32,767 for an assigned short

  • Integers

    • Integers are integer numbers in an range of -2,147,483,643 to 2,147,483,647 for signed integers
